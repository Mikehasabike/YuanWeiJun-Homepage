I am Weijun Yuan, Assistant Instructional Professor of Sociology in the Master of Arts Program in Social Sciences at The University of Chicago. I received my Ph.D. in Sociology from the University of California, Irvine.

I am a sociologist of collective action, networks, and organization. My main line of research focuses on the dynamics and outcomes of collective action. I use digital trace data and quantitative and computational methods to study political processes leading to social change. My work has appeared in Social Forces, Sociological Forum, and Research in Social Movements, Conflicts and Change. My research has received awards from the American Sociological Association and funding from The BIGSSS Computational Social Science Summer Schools and Research Incubator, China Scholarship Council, Institute for Humane Studies, Summer Institute in Computational Social Sciences, among others.

My methodological expertise encompasses network analysis, natural language processing, regression analysis, causal inference, comparative historical analysis, and qualitative comparative analysis (QCA). I was a project leader and invited speaker at The BIGSSS Computational Social Science Summer Schools and Research Incubator in Bremen, Germany.

Before coming to graduate school, I worked as a researcher and project coordinator at The Arias Foundation for Peace and Human Progress, Costa Rica. I have also served as the founding coordinator for The Center for Developing Country Studies (DCS) at Tsinghua University, Beijing. I received my Bachelor's degree from Tsinghua University and my Master's degree from the University of California, San Diego.
